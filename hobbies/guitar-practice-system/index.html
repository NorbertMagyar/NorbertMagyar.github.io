Weekly Practice System for Guitar

My main objective was consistency, not maximum session length. I split practice into short blocks that fit busy weekdays: technique, rhythm drills, and repertoire. The structure reduced decision fatigue and made it easier to start every day.

Each block has a concrete target. Technique focuses on one pattern at a fixed tempo, rhythm focuses on subdivision stability with a metronome, and repertoire focuses on one difficult passage instead of full-song playthroughs.

I keep a simple log of date, duration, and one sentence about what improved or stalled. That tiny feedback loop made progress measurable and helped adjust weekly priorities.

The system is still evolving, but the biggest outcome so far is that practice feels much more sustainable during high-workload weeks.
